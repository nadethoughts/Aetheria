## 🧙‍♀️ The Witches’ Hut: Ritual of the Ember Key

### 🏚️ Location:

A twisted, vine-choked hut deep in the Burned Wood, built atop the ruins of an ancient La Pelu shrine. The hut is surrounded by red-leafed trees and warped totems. Inside, a massive cauldron bubbles with glowing embers and blood-red mist.

### 🔥 The Ritual:

The hags are not trying to resurrect Vorath directly—they are attempting to **forge the Ember Key**, a magical construct that will allow them to bypass the divine locks on the Time Turner.

To do this, they need:

- **Toadwin’s blood**, as he is a “soul of balance” tied to the Llano.
- **The cauldron**, which channels the corrupted essence of the Fire Walker totems.
- **A fragment of a La Pelu relic**, stolen from a ruined shrine (perhaps a broken sunstone or shard of the Mirror of Ashes).

### 🧪 Ritual Mechanics:

- The cauldron is the ritual focus. It glows brighter as the ritual progresses.
- Toadwin is suspended above it, unconscious, bound in thorny vines and fire glyphs.
- The hags chant in Infernal and Goblin, invoking the “Unmaker’s Flame.”

### 🧭 Narrative Stakes:

- If the ritual completes, the Ember Key will be forged.
- This key will allow the hags to **breach the Time Turner’s seal** and begin unraveling the Llano’s protections.
- Vorath’s influence will begin to seep into the world—not as a body, but as a **corruptive force** that alters memory, time, and truth.

## ⚔️ Encounter Setup: “The Cauldron of Unmaking”

### Enemies:

- **Velkra**, Hag Matron of the Fire Walker Coven (ritual caster)
- **2 Fire Walker Goblin Brutes** (protecting the hut)
- **1 Goblin Hexer** (maintaining the cauldron’s magic)
- **Environmental Hazard**: The cauldron pulses every round, dealing fire or psychic damage in a 10-ft radius unless disabled.

### Objectives:

- Rescue Toadwin before the ritual completes
- Disable or destroy the cauldron
- Defeat or drive off the hags

### Optional Twist:

- If the party interrupts the ritual mid-casting, the cauldron explodes in a burst of temporal energy, causing a **brief time distortion** (e.g., players relive a round, or enemies act twice).
