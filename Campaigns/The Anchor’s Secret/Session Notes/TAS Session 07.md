# The Anchor's Secret - Session 7 Recap

**"Racing the Tide"**

---

## What Happened

### **The Race to the Temple**

After defeating the Stormcaller leader and rescuing three Iron Tide soldiers, you faced a critical choice about the unconscious priestess. Jezrek tried to identify the enchantment—some kind of magical sleep that he couldn't break. Skirk suggested the direct approach (a good smack), but it didn't work.

Unable to wake her and with pirates closing in, you entrusted her to the Iron Tide soldiers and bolted for the Temple of Miraine.

**Your harrowing journey:**

### **The Collapsed Bridge**

A 50-foot-wide river with destroyed bridge, support pillars jutting from rushing water.

**Jezrek and Skirk's moment to shine:** Using scattered lumber and stones, they constructed a working bridge with incredible teamwork. Skirk broke wood where needed, Jezrek worked his tinker's magic. With a +11 to the roll and Skirk's assistance, they built it in minutes—and it was _nicer than the original_.

Then Jezrek infused the bridge and _folded it into his pack_. Because why leave it for the pirates?

### **Navigate the Mists**

Thick fog that could have hidden dangers (steam elementals lurked here before).

**The group attempted a Survival check** to navigate:

- Several middling rolls (8s and such)
- But a 19 and a 20 saved you!

**Result:** You got a bit turned around in the mist, but Jezrek's star compass helped point the way. No combat, just lost a little time. Pirates gained some ground.

### **The Pearlbound Perimeter**

As you approached the temple, you saw it: a massive **coral dome** covering the entire structure, glowing with an orange-ish hue. And surrounding it—**far more Pearlbound than you could possibly fight**. 50+ undead, circling the temple like moths to flame.

**Xander led the way.** As he approached, the Pearlbound simply... parted. Like the Red Sea. They turned their hollow eyes toward him but made no move to attack.

The rest of you stuck _very_ close to Xander. (Skirk walking literally underneath his legs like a dog.)

### **The Coralite Dome**

A complete sphere of pure Coralite—magical coral—encasing the Temple of Miraine. Lil John recognized it immediately as the valuable crafting material he'd been seeking.

**Skirk's solution:** "There's only one way to fix that." He hooked his anchor into the lattice and with a DC 18 Athletics check (rolled 19!), _ripped a hole straight through it_.

**Xander's discovery:** Using Perception (rolled 25!), he found that:

- It's actually a complete _sphere_, not just a dome
- The center is underground
- There's a _seam_ you could widen without destroying it

**Skirk squeezed through the hole first:** "This is creepy. Come in here, I don't want to be in here alone."

The rest of you found the seam and widened it like a tent flap. Xander then cast Mending to patch Skirk's hole—slowing down the pirates.

### **Inside the Temple**

The Temple of Miraine sits in a shallow pool of seawater (barely ankle-deep). You waded across without incident and entered the grand hall.

**The scene:**

- A quiet hall of pearl, cool and heavy with salt
- An altar shaped like a boat's keel with glowing seawater
- Behind it: Miraine's symbol carved into the wall—a shield with waves inside

**Mistral's map marked it clearly.** Instructions:

1. Stand in front of the symbol
2. Place your palm on the center of the shield
3. Speak Miraine's name
4. Press firmly

**Skirk:** "Can I rip the shield off the wall?"  
**DM:** "It's carved INTO the wall."  
**Skirk:** "Damn."

Mistral followed the instructions. The stone gave slightly. The water in the altar began spiraling down through hidden channels. A low rumble. The shield split down the center, the wall parted, and **cold air rolled out from darkness beyond**.

Stone steps descended into shadow—**the Gloamways**.

Before heading down, Jezrek dipped his flail in the special Miraine holy water (getting that lightning damage ready).

## **The Chamber of the Pearl**

You descended the winding stone staircase deep underground and emerged into:

**A flooded chamber:**

- Water waist-deep (difficult)
- Pale blue bioluminescent light
- Four levers along the walls, each marked with a symbol:
    - Closest to entrance: **Flame** and **Sun**
    - Far end: Two more (you couldn't see them yet)
- At the far end: A raised dais with a fountain and empty pedestal
- At the base of the dais: A massive **Pearl**, nearly the size of a man's head
- Shards of Coralite littering the floor beneath the water
- A dull _thumping_ sound in the background

You stepped forward cautiously, spreading out...

**Then the water moved.**

**"You all get one bonus action before we roll initiative."**

**Your responses:**

- **Jezrek:** Created a ROWBOAT beneath himself using his Replicate Magic Item (Orb of Creation). He's now out of the water, in a tiny boat.
- **Xander:** Sprouted spectral angel wings (Otherworldly Guise), gaining a fly speed equal to his walking speed. "Oh, Miraine... I could really use your help right now."
- **Skirk:** Rage
- **Mistral:** Nothing declared

### **Initiative 50: Divine Onslaught**

Before you could even roll initiative, **the Knight appeared from the water itself** and executed a devastating opening move:

It teleported adjacent to _each of you_ and made one attack:

- **Xander:** 19 to hit → 15 damage (8 slashing, 7 radiant)
- **Mistral:** 16 to hit (exactly his AC) → 15 damage
- **Skirk:** 23 to hit → 11 damage (used Rage to halve the physical)
- **Jezrek:** 21 to hit → 15 damage

Then it teleported to a position in the middle of the room.

## **Key Discoveries**

1. **The valves control the Knight's power**: Opening valves drained the water and reduced its regeneration from 50 HP/round to just 10 HP/round
2. **Elemental damage activates the valves**: You need to deal the matching element damage within 10 feet of a valve to unlock it
3. **The Knight hesitated against Xander**: Something about attacking him felt wrong to it
4. **The sword was a pirate's weapon**: The cutlass reshaped by water magic suggests the Knight might have once been one of the pirates who tried to steal the Pearl before
5. **Difficult terrain was KEY**: The flooded room was a massive tactical disadvantage until you solved the puzzle
