## 2. WHAT THIS MEANS FOR THE KNIGHT

### With Regen 25 (Flooded)
- Effective DPR ≈ **49**
- 125 HP ÷ 49 ≈ **2.5–3 rounds**

⚠️ This is now _dangerously fast_.
### During Drain (Regen 5)
- Effective DPR ≈ **69**
- Knight evaporates in **1–2 rounds**

👉 **Conclusion:**  
Without additional pressure, **burning the Knight is now mathematically viable**, especially with good play and prone/AC debuffs.

So we must make **ignoring valves hurt**.

---
## 3. KEEP THE VALVES WORTH SOLVING (SIMPLE FIXES)
These are **binary**, easy to remember, and require _no extra math tracking_.

---
### 🔑 RECOMMENDATION #1 (Most Important)
### **Regen Happens on BOTH Initiatives**
Instead of once per round:
- Regen **25** at the start of **each Knight turn** while flooded
With two initiatives:
- **Total regen = 50 per round**
Now the math becomes:
- Party DPR ~74
- Effective DPR after regen ≈ **24**
- Fight lasts **5+ rounds** unless valves are addressed
This single change restores the puzzle’s importance immediately.

✔️ No new tracking  
✔️ No new conditions  
✔️ Very intuitive (“the tide keeps restoring it”)

---
### 🔑 RECOMMENDATION #2
### **Valves Only Drain at End of Round**
When the third valve is opened:
- Drain begins **at end of round**
- Regen reduction applies **next round**

This prevents:
- “We open the last valve mid-round and delete it immediately”

Still fair, still clean.

---
### 🔑 RECOMMENDATION #3
### **Valve Pressure in Phase 2 (Already Fits Your Design)**
You already have this, but make it explicit:
> If **no valve is opened in a round**, the Knight prioritizes **valve-adjacent targets** on its Low Initiative.

This is a _tactical incentive_, not a mechanical penalty.

---
### OPTIONAL (Use Only If Needed)
### **Flooded Empowerment**
While flooded:
- The Knight gains **advantage on its first attack each initiative**
This increases threat without changing math complexity.

---
## FINAL RECOMMENDED CONFIG (CLEAN VERSION)
- Regen **25 per initiative** while flooded (50/round)
- Regen **5 per initiative** while drained (10/round)
- Drain begins at **end of round**
- Phases unchanged
- Valves always relevant
